Getting into web development could not be easier. There are a ton of free tools. It is rare that something is so easy to get into and the way you get into it is absolutely free.

A text editor is the first thing necessary to get started. It can’t be just any text editor though. You want to pick an editor that fits the way you work and has a library of plugins to progress with your skill level on your web development path. I have come across a few text editors. The list includes NotePad++, Brackets.io, Atom.io, Sublime Text (free, but not free), metapad, Komodo Edit. As I said before, these editors are free. You don’t have to pay to download, install, or use them in your environment.

Next, you want to be, or get familiar with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. There are videos available on YouTube, as well as some great resources available through the library system and Lynda.com.

If you are familiar you could ‘level-up’ by finding and using and Integrated Development Environment (IDE). IDE’s like JetBrains Webstorm, MS Visual Studio, Firefox and firebug (recently became FDT), NetBeans, and Komodo are just a few that can be accessed for free. You may have to register to be able to download the product.
